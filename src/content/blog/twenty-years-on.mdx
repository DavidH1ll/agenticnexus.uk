I keep coming back to a line I wrote on my GitHub profile a while back: *IT Pro, software engineering, security, Python, Rust.* Four commas, four pivots, all of them real. None of them abrupt. Twenty years of running enterprise systems is, when you stand back from it, twenty years of building systems too — the difference is mostly which layer of the stack you're allowed to touch.

The thing I didn't expect about learning to code in my forties was how much of it felt familiar. Patching a Windows estate, designing a call-centre network, running a five-account ISMS programme — those are all engineering jobs with feedback loops. They just don't ship as `git push`.

## What changed

The 100-days-of-Python project forced a pace I hadn't kept in a long time. One thing a day, in public, no going back. It's an unforgiving format: you ship something that works or you fall behind. By the end I had a working familiarity with Flask, Pandas, scikit-learn, the boring infrastructure of a real web app, and a much better mental model of what "production" actually means for a small project.

What I don't have yet is the systems-level fluency I want. That's the Rust work. Different tool, different shape of problem, slower pace — and that's fine.

## What this site is

This is the public part of the journey. The case studies document finished work. The credentials list what's on the wall. This journal is for the in-between: the things I'm reading, the things I'm trying, the small notes that don't fit anywhere else.

If any of it is useful to someone else going the same route, even better.
